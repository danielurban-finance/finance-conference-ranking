Finance Conference Ranking

Rank the major finance conferences by the share of presented papers later published in a journal set you choose — in the same spirit as Hou, Smajlbegović & Urban (2025, Journal of Empirical Finance 84, 101652). A presented paper is counted as published when it can be linked to a journal article — by matching paper titles and author names, and corroborating with the acknowledgement footnotes in which authors thank the conferences where the work was presented. All computation runs in your browser.

About

Daniel Urban · urban@ese.eur.nl · sites.google.com/view/danielurban

This tool extends, in the same spirit, the conference-quality analysis of Hou, Smajlbegović and Urban (2025, Journal of Empirical Finance 84, 101652), letting you vary the journal set, conference years and match confidence interactively.

Status & limitations. The matching is currently fully automated — no match is hand-checked or hand-coded yet, so some publications are missed and some are matched in error. The main known weakness is title changes: when a paper's title changes substantially between its conference (working-paper) version and publication, a title-based match can fail. The acknowledgement-footnote and former-title links recover many such cases, but they remain the leading source of missed matches. Results should be read as indicative, not definitive.

Feedback & inclusion. Feedback is very welcome. Organisers of finance conferences who would like their conference added are warmly invited to get in touch.
